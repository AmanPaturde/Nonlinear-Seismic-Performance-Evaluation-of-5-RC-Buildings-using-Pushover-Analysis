# Nonlinear-Seismic-Performance-Evaluation-of-5-RC-Buildings-using-Pushover-Analysis
### Pushover Analysis in SAP2000 | FEMA-356 & ATC-40 

## Project Summary
Comparative seismic performance assessment of 5 reinforced concrete buildings using SAP2000, including linear, modal, response spectrum, and nonlinear pushover analyses.

<img width="678" height="874" alt="1781848285798" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/116fd3b5-3b1b-4073-9047-bc96d0ca5bd5" />

## Objectives:
1. Compare regular RC buildings
2. Study building height
3. Study irregularity
4. Study shear walls
5. Compare linear vs nonlinear behaviour

## Some interesting observations:
- Vertical setbacks significantly altered damage progression and hinge concentration.
- Shea walls dramatically improved stiffness and drift control.
- Structural strength alone did not guarantee better seismic performance.
- Different structural systems achieved earthquake resistance through very different combinations of stiffness, ductility, and overstrength.

# The biggest lesson from this project was that seismic design is not simply about making a structure stronger. Understanding how and where a building yields can be just as important as understanding how much load it can carry.

## Future Scope:
# The present study may be extended through the following investigations:
1. Nonlinear time-history analysis using scaled ground motion records compatible with the IS 1893 design spectrum to evaluate the dynamic response of the investigated structures under realistic earthquake excitation.
2. Development of fragility curves using Incremental Dynamic Analysis (IDA) to quantify the probability of exceeding Immediate Occupancy (IO), Life Safety (LS), and Collapse Prevention (CP) performance levels.
3. Investigation of soil-structure interaction effects through flexible foundation modeling and comparison with the fixed-base assumptions adopted in the present study.
4. Inclusion of nonlinear shear wall hinge behavior and distributed plasticity models to obtain a more realistic representation of wall damage progression.
